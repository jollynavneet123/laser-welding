Laser Welding Process Details

Laser welding can operate in two common modes: conduction mode and keyhole mode. Conduction mode gives shallow, smooth welds, while keyhole mode gives deeper penetration due to higher power density (TWI, n.d.).

Main Equipment

  • Laser source (fiber, CO2, or solid-state laser)
  • Beam delivery system (optics/fiber cable)
  • Focusing head and nozzle
  • Robot or motion table
  • Shielding gas setup
  • Workholding fixture and safety enclosure

Common Parameters

  • Laser power (W or kW)
  • Travel speed (mm/s)
  • Focal position
  • Beam spot size
  • Shielding gas type and flow rate
  • Joint fit-up and gap control

Step-by-Step Workflow

  1. Clean and prepare base material surfaces.
  2. Clamp parts with proper alignment and root gap.
  3. Set welding parameters and perform test coupon weld.
  4. Run production weld with continuous monitoring.
  5. Inspect visual quality and dimensional accuracy.
  6. Perform required NDT or destructive tests as specified.

Advantages and Limitations

Advantages Limitations
High speed and high precision. High equipment cost and setup complexity.
Narrow heat affected zone, less distortion. Joint fit-up tolerance can be strict.
Excellent for automation and repeatability. Requires strong safety controls for laser exposure.
