Safety and Quality Control

Laser welding uses intense optical energy and can create hazards such as eye injury, skin burns, fumes, and electrical risks. Controls include enclosed work cells, proper PPE, ventilation, and clear safety procedures (Occupational Safety and Health Administration [OSHA], n.d.).

Important Safety Practices

  • Use approved laser safety eyewear and face protection.
  • Operate within enclosed or interlocked welding stations.
  • Maintain local exhaust ventilation for fume control.
  • Follow lockout/tagout and machine guarding procedures.
  • Train workers on emergency stop and incident response.

Quality Checks

  • Visual inspection for bead continuity and surface defects.
  • Dimensional checks for distortion or mismatch.
  • Macro-etch or cross-section analysis for penetration depth.
  • NDT options where required (e.g., radiography, UT).
  • Documented parameter records for traceability.

Common Weld Defects

Porosity

Gas trapped in the weld metal can reduce strength and appearance.

Lack of Fusion

Insufficient energy or poor joint fit-up may prevent full bonding.

Cracking

Can occur due to high restraint, contamination, or poor parameter selection.
